About Me

I'm a budding writer from Manchester, England. Growing up, I had a bit of a rocky journey with feeling like I never quite fit in. I felt like I saw things differently and couldn't bring myself to just conform to a group. It was tough, but I learned a lot from those experiences.

I started writing what later turned into my first book when I was 12, primarily because I couldn't keep the perceptions I had on the world bottled up any longer. Writing down my thoughts and ideas was like therapy for me, a way to make sense of things. And when I shared them with a few mates, they found it helpful too. That's when I realised that my early struggles weren't just obstacles; they were tools I could use to help others through my writing.

I'm on a mission to make a positive difference with my words, especially for those who feel out of place in this world, to show them they're not alone. My career has been all about helping people. I've worked in a psychiatric hospital, a probation hostel, a children's care home and SEN schools. All these experiences have given me a wider view of what people go through and have let me reach even more people with my message. I believe my angle and way of thinking is unique and I have found a way to harness that to truly help people in every walk of life for which I am extremely proud.

I know what it's like to not feel heard or understood and I want to spread the word that if you feel the same, you should embrace that and not shy away and conform like we are so often taught to do. I hope my writing can help people truly be themselves in a world that is always trying to put us into boxes.

Books

The Policy

The Policy is a 30-page story that asks one simple question: why do we lie so much? It follows a man whose carefully crafted life of deception begins to unravel, forcing him to confront the uncomfortable truths he’s long avoided. Through a chance encounter on a storm-soaked train ride, he begins to see the cost of dishonesty—and the freedom in facing reality. This isn’t a self-help manual with steps or bullet points. It’s a raw, thought-provoking reflection on the oldest lesson we never truly live by: tell the truth.

Alienated

Alienated is a powerful, thought-provoking book about finding clarity and connection in a world that often feels isolating. Through raw personal insights and striking metaphors, Sam shares how he turned a lifelong sense of separation into strength—and how you can too. It’s a guide for anyone who’s ever felt out of place, offering a new lens to see the world and yourself more clearly.

Perception

Perception challenges the life you’ve grown used to — the constant worrying, the pressure to fit in, and the feeling that your true passions are always on hold. It’s a book for anyone who feels out of place, weighed down by stress, or searching for something more. Written for the outcasts and the overlooked, it offers a new way to see yourself and the world — one that makes room for authenticity, hope, and a happier way of living.

Robin's bench

Robin’s Bench follows Adam, a young man exhausted from pretending to be someone he’s not. Seeking peace in the Peak District, he meets Robin — a quiet, grounded figure who spends his nights beneath the stars.Through a series of honest, philosophical conversations, Adam begins to break down his walls and rediscover himself. This novel is a gentle yet powerful reminder that healing begins when we stop running from who we are.
